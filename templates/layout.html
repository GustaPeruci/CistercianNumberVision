About Cistercian Numerals

Cistercian numerals, also called monk's numerals, were developed by Cistercian monks in the early 13th century. This numeral system is remarkable for its ability to encode numbers from 1 to 9999 in a single symbol.

How Cistercian Numerals Work:

The system uses a vertical stem with different marks in four quadrants:

  • Bottom Right: Units (1-9)
  • Top Right: Tens (10-90)
  • Bottom Left: Hundreds (100-900)
  • Top Left: Thousands (1000-9000)

Each digit from 1-9 has a unique symbol that is placed in the appropriate quadrant to represent its place value.

Examples:
  • 1: A horizontal line extending from the stem to the right at the bottom
  • 10: A horizontal line extending from the stem to the right at the top
  • 100: A horizontal line extending from the stem to the left at the bottom
  • 1000: A horizontal line extending from the stem to the left at the top

This application allows you to convert between regular numbers and their Cistercian numeral representations, as well as recognize Cistercian numerals using computer vision technology.
